Plus 2 course description


Plus two or +2 or Ten plus Two in Nepal has almost equal number of pass outs when compared to that of SLC students, who are equally confused as well. When a student completes SLC there is only handful of options but when it comes to a +2 (plus two) student there is an entire horizon of alternatives. Although there is a sense of delightfulness after completion of high school, there also remains a big chaos of what is to come next in terms of education. There are plenty of options when it comes to doing undergraduate in Nepal or going abroad for the same.

Science

Science is the base for the students who want to study medical or engineering’s in coming future.It’s a two years course. It’s a course designed for those types of students who have interest on technical things. After joining these courses they have broad range for their career. They can become doctors, engineers, nurse, pilot, pharmacist, etc. The government has provided many further studies for the students so now days this course is chosen by many people. Students taking biological group can study medicines and incase they can’t become a doctor they can study B.Sc. in micro-biology, pharmacology, medical technology, genetic engineering, bio-chemistry etc. and the students who belongs to the physical and math’s groups they can take courses of engineering like mechanical engineering, computer engineering, or other different engineering faculties.

Management

Under this faculty the students mostly study about managing different areas. Business studies, accounting, trade etc. are management subjects. It is also a two year course which develops managerial skills in the students. Likewise science English and Nepali are compulsory subjects. Hotel management, computer science, marketing and business math’s are optional subject. Students taking business subjects like accounting and business studies further on can study BBA, BBS. Computer students can go on and study business IT, and the students taking hotel management can further study BHM and BTTM. The aim of this course is to make students eligible for office manager, business owner or corporate employee.

A-levels course is affiliated to Cambridge University, UK. It has gained popularity amongst Nepali students because in A-levels, the student does not really have to go through courses that they do not feel like studying. Even though the courses available in the UK have much more variety, quite a selection is on offer from the British Council and numerous colleges here. FORESTRY

orestry is one of the fields that hold good future prospects for the students who have completed S.L.C. This subject is also a noble venture into forest conservation and constraining the mounting environmental degradation. Nepal has two institutions for forestry – one in Hetauda and another in Pokhara, both affiliated to the Tribhuvan University, offering certificate and bachelors level studies. The course provides advanced training in forestry and natural resource, conservation and management. Additional responsibilities include forestry research and development of technology suitable for Nepal. The campuses of the Institute of Forestry have laboratories, woodlots, arboreta, horticulture research farms, computer and other facilities.

Fine Art

Fine Art has a long history in Nepal. The first school established was ‘Art School’ in 1903. The subject is the best for the creative students. There are various Fine Arts colleges in and outside the valley that provide intermediate courses in Fine Arts. The Intermediate has classes on the basics of drawing and painting, and sculpture. English and Nepali are the compulsory subjects. While some colleges don’t offer the graphic part of the designing, some like Institute of Fine Arts, at Ganabahal, a newly opened Fine Arts institution, has such offering. Further into the course, classes on classical instruments, classical dances, textile designing and so forth are available
